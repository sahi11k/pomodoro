    What is the Pomodoro Technique?

    The Pomodoro Technique, created by Francesco Cirillo, is a time management method that helps you stay focused and productive. Named after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer ("pomodoro" in Italian) that Cirillo used as a university student, this technique breaks work into focused 25-minute intervals called pomodori. Each pomodoro is followed by a 5-minute break, and after completing four pomodori, you take a longer 15-30 minute break. This structured approach helps prevent burnout while maintaining concentration throughout your work or study sessions. ‚Äî Wikipedia

    How to use the Pomodoro Timer?

    1. Add a task you want to complete.
    2. Set the Pomodoro timer (typically 25 minutes).
    3. Work on the task.
    4. End work when the timer rings and take a short break (typically 5 to 10 minutes).
    5. Go back to Step 2 and repeat until you complete four pomodori (plural of pomodoro).
    6. After four pomodori are done, take a long break (typically 15 to 20 minutes) instead of a short break.
    7. Once the long break is finished, return to step 2.
